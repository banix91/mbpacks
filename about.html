Demonstrating the heart of Christ

The story of the Good Samaritan found in Luke 10:30-37 shows all of us the heart of Christ when it comes to serving those that are victims of crime. Then Jesus said to him, “Go and do likewise.” What an amazing command from Jesus. When we stand with and encourage victims of gun violence, we clearly demonstrate the heart of Christ by showing mercy to those that have been wounded physically and emotionally.

Our ministry goal is simple…

to follow the command of Jesus, “Go and do likewise.” Mercy Backpacks are filled with much needed toiletries to shock trauma patients preparing to return home from treatment at the University of Maryland Medical Center.

Who We Are

Mercy Backpacks is a ministry to victims of gun violence. Since 2017, we have reached out to victims of gun violence through our partnership with the Violence Prevention Program at the Shock Trauma Center University of Maryland Medical Center.We have distributed over 230 Mercy Backpacks to victims of gun violence.

Mercy Backpack Founder

Ronald Nix

Ronald Nix, founder of Mercy Backpacks, A Ministry to Victims of Gun Violence, served as Church Coordinator, Washington D.C Chapter of Neighbors Who Care, a ministry to victims of crime and subsidiary of Prison Fellowship Ministries. Ronald also served as Area Director, Maryland, District of Columbia, Delaware and West Virginia for Prison Fellowship Ministries. Ronald is a graduate of the University of Baltimore, Washington Bible College and a Center for Urban Families Fellow, Practitioners Leadership Institute, Baltimore, Maryland. Ronald and his wife are members of the First Baptist Church of Glenarden in Prince George’s County, Maryland. Ronald Nix retired as a Grant Manager of Responsible Fatherhood programs from the State of Maryland, Child Support Adminsitration.
